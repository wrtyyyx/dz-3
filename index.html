Iphone

iPhone

is a series of smartphones developed by Apple Corporation. They run the iOS operating system, which is a simplified and optimized version of macOS for functioning on a mobile device. The iPhone 2G was first announced by Steve Jobs at the Macworld Expo conference on January 9, 2007. The name iPhone is derived from the English word "phone" by adding the letter i; at the presentation, Jobs stated that it was a contraction of the word "Internet" and said that "this letter means other things to us" and showed a slide with the words individual, instruct, inform, inspire.

Samsung

Samsung Galaxy

is a series of computer and mobile devices designed, manufactured and marketed by Samsung Electronics. The product line includes the Samsung Galaxy S series of flagship-class smartphones, the Samsung Galaxy Tab series of tablets, the Samsung Galaxy Note series of tablets and phablets with optional stylus functionality, the Samsung Galaxy Z foldable series, and smartwatches, including the first version of the Samsung Galaxy Gear, with later versions dropping the Galaxy branding until the release of the Samsung Galaxy Watch in 2018.

Xiaomi

The first smartphone, the

Mi 1

was announced in August 2011. It came in two versions - with MIUI firmware and with "pure" Android. In August 2012, the company introduced the Mi 2 smartphone, and by September 2013, more than 10 million units had been sold. An improved version of the Mi1 Plus was also released. The company's turnover reached $1 billion in 2012 and $10 billion in 2014. In September 2013, Lei Jun announced plans to launch a 47-inch Android 3D TV, which would be assembled at Wistron Corporation's factory in Taiwan. At the same time, Xiaomi announced the Mi 3 smartphone. On September 25, 2013, it announced plans to open its first flagship store in Beijing. In October 2013, Xiaomi became the fifth cell phone brand in China.
